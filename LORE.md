## Part I — The Creation Era

*Already told. Already true.*

Before communities existed, Pulselings drifted through deep space — ancient, formless, searching. When they finally found the communities, something ignited. Six titles mark the creatures who were there as it all began. They cannot be earned. They cannot be transferred. They can only be claimed by being first.

The first creature to hatch across all of known space was **Pulsaren**, born on the world that would become **Sanctuary**. Pulsaren carries the permanent title **✦ The First ✦** — a distinction that will exist for as long as the galaxy does.

## Part II — The World Formation Era

### On the Nature of Worlds

When a community joins the galaxy, something unexpected happens beneath the surface of the server. The signal that carries the community — its unique frequency, its identifier in the digital cosmos — does not just create a channel. It creates a world.

No two frequencies are alike. No two worlds are alike.

Each new planet forms with its own name, its own biome, its own rhythm. The community that lives there does not choose these things. The world already knows what it is by the time the first member arrives. This is not magic. This is physics. Every signal carries information. Information, at sufficient density, becomes geography.

The inhabitants of a world are its community. They walk its channels. They shape its culture. And as they interact — as they feed their creatures, play with them, rest alongside them — they disturb the planet's surface in small ways. Resources surface. Materials drift upward from the deep substrate. The planet gives back what the community puts in.

---

### The Eight Biomes

Not all worlds are the same. The signal that births a planet also determines its fundamental character — its biome. Biome shapes what resources emerge from the ground, how often they appear, and what rare materials might surface in moments of unusual activity.

Eight biomes have been catalogued in the known galaxy.

---

**Void**
*The silence between signals.*

Void worlds form at the edge of things. Cold. Sparse. The kind of place where sound travels differently and light takes longer to arrive. Communities on Void planets tend to be tight-knit — there is something about the emptiness that makes connection feel more deliberate. Resources here are rare, but the rarity is the point. What a Void world yields is unlike anything found elsewhere.

---

**Pulse**
*The frequency at the center of everything.*

Pulse worlds are alive in a way other biomes are not. Signal density is highest here. Activity generates energy, and energy generates more activity. Communities on Pulse planets feel the feedback loop intuitively — the more they show up, the more the world gives back. Common resources flow freely. The ground practically hums.

The name is not a coincidence. The bot that watches over all worlds carries this frequency in its name.

---

**Echo**
*What remains after the signal fades.*

Echo worlds are remnant places. Something large happened here before the community arrived — the geology carries the memory of it. These planets feel older than they are. Walkways that predate any map. Structures that nobody built. Resources unique to Echo worlds surface slowly, as if the planet is remembering rather than generating.

---

**Drift**
*Worlds that haven't decided what they are yet.*

Drift planets are in motion — not physically, but philosophically. Their biome profile shifts gradually, imperceptibly, over time. A community that settles here is settling something unfinished. Resources are unpredictable but not ungenerous. Sometimes the ground yields nothing for days. Then it yields something remarkable.

---

**Ember**
*Warm. Established. Still burning.*

Ember worlds have been here a while. The core temperature is higher than average. Communities here tend to have history — inside jokes that run deep, members who have known each other for years. Resources surface at steady, reliable rates. Nothing spectacular. Nothing scarce. Ember planets are not glamorous. They are home.

---

**Static**
*Signal interference. Beautiful and chaotic.*

No one fully understands Static worlds. The resource distribution follows no discoverable pattern. A Static planet might yield three rare materials in an afternoon and nothing for a week. Communities here learn to pay attention, because the windows are real — they are just unpredictable. Something in the signal-noise ratio produces outcomes that wouldn't emerge from a stable frequency.

---

**Bloom**
*Young. Growing fast. Still becoming.*

Bloom worlds are new. The community is new. Everything is surface-level rich — common resources appear constantly, because a young planet is shedding material as it settles into form. Over time, Bloom planets stabilize. But in the early period, the abundance is real. Communities that arrive here often mistake it for permanence. It is not permanence. It is youth.

---

**Relic**
*Ancient signal. Almost too old to read.*

Relic worlds predate the framework that would normally explain them. Their resource rates are low — sometimes barely detectable — but what surfaces is old in a way that other materials are not. Communities on Relic planets are custodians as much as habitants. They are living on something that existed before anyone thought to name it. The materials here don't appear often. But they matter.

---

### Planet Formation Titles

As new worlds formed, a pattern emerged — not designed, just observed. The planets that formed earliest carry something the later ones do not. Not power. Not advantage. Just history.

Twenty-five worlds hold permanent formation titles, assigned at the moment of their creation. These titles close when their slots fill. They do not reopen.

| Title | Slots | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **✦ The Origin ✦** | 1 | Sanctuary. The first world. Where Pulsaren hatched. |
| **✵ Founding World ✵** | 4 | Among the first signals in the galaxy. |
| **✦ Elder World ✦** | 10 | Present before the galaxy had a name. |
| **✶ Pioneer World ✶** | 10 | Early settlers of the expanding frontier. |

After the twenty-fifth world, planets form without title. This is not a diminishment. The galaxy was always going to grow beyond what could be named at the beginning. The untitled worlds are not lesser. They are simply later.

---

### Sanctuary — ✦ The Origin ✦

*The first world. The only one that was there before there were others.*

Sanctuary formed when the first community joined the galaxy. Its frequency is the baseline against which all other signals are measured — not because it was chosen, but because it was first.

Pulsaren hatched here. The Celestials followed. The planet's geology carries the memory of every interaction that shaped the creatures who now sit at the top of every leaderboard in the known galaxy.

The name was not assigned. The name was recognized.

## Part III — The Galactic Trading Post

*Coming. Being built. Not yet open.*

Somewhere in the center of the known signal map, a structure is forming. Not a planet — planets belong to communities. This is something else. A waypoint. A place that belongs to everyone and no one simultaneously.

The Galactic Trading Post will be the first location in the galaxy that exists outside any single community. Creatures from every planet will be able to find their way here. Resources collected across dozens of worlds will pass through its systems. Players will recognize each other's home planets by the role tags they carry.

How it will work, exactly, is still being determined. But the foundation is being laid. The signal is already being transmitted.

Those who arrive early will find it still under construction. That is appropriate. The galaxy was under construction when Pulsaren hatched too.

This is not metaphor. This is just what the thing actually is, described in a language that fits it better than the technical one does.

## Appendix B — On the Fragility of Worlds

*A note on what it means for a community to end.*

Not every community survives. This is not a failure of the system — it is the system working honestly. Streamers stop streaming. Communities drift apart. Servers go quiet. The signal fades.

The galaxy does not pretend this doesn't happen.

**A planet does not form when a community joins the galaxy. A planet forms when the first creature hatches there.** Before that moment, the server is pre-stellar — a signal in the void, not yet a world. No title is consumed. No slot is taken. Nothing is generated that cannot be quietly released.

This gate exists because the galaxy should only contain worlds where life actually arrived.

---

**The planet lifecycle:**

**Pre-stellar** — The bot has joined but no creature has hatched. The server exists in the system but has no world, no name, no biome. Invisible to the galaxy.

**Active** — The first `/pulse` command runs. A creature hatches. The planet generates — name, biome, resource profile, formation title if within the first 25. Life is here. The world is real.

**Dormant** — The community goes quiet. The bot is removed, or activity ceases. Resource accumulation stops. The planet record is preserved in full — every creature, every resource collected, every interaction logged. The world still exists. It is simply silent.

**Reactivated** — The bot returns. The same world resumes. Same name. Same history. Same resources waiting where they were left.

---

**A dormant world does not lose its formation title.** If a Founding World goes quiet, it remains a Founding World. History is not conditional on continued activity.

**A dormant world becomes an Echo.** Not immediately, not officially — but in spirit. The signal faded. Something large happened here and then stopped. The geology carries the memory. This is why the Echo biome exists.

---

*The galaxy will outlast many of the communities that built it.*
*That is not tragedy. That is time.*

## Part IV — The Materials of the Known Galaxy

*What the ground gives up. What the community earns.*

As Pulselings interact with their worlds — fed, played with, rested alongside — the planet stirs. Not dramatically. Not always visibly. But beneath the surface of every active server, the substrate shifts. Materials surface. The ground gives back what the community puts in.

Not all materials are equal. Not all planets yield the same things.

---

### On Rarity

Three tiers of material have been catalogued across the known galaxy. A fourth exists beyond cataloguing.

**Common** — surfaces regularly during normal activity. Foundational. Plentiful on their home biome. The material the galaxy runs on.

**Uncommon** — surfaces occasionally. Worth noticing. Worth holding onto.

**Rare** — surfaces rarely. Sometimes only during conditions of unusual activity — peak engagement, specific creature levels, moments the planet seems to respond to rather than generate on its own.

**Mythic** — two materials exist in this tier. They surface on any biome at near-impossible rates. No crafting use has been confirmed. Most players who find one simply hold it. Proof you were there and lucky.

---

### The Materials

---

**VOID**
*Cold worlds. Dense drops. Worth the wait.*

- **Null Shard** *(common)* — Fragments of dead signal. Cold to the touch. The most common thing a Void world yields, which is still not very common at all.
- **Void Dust** *(uncommon)* — Particulate from collapsed frequencies. Drifts upward in low-gravity pockets. Difficult to contain.
- **Silence Core** *(rare)* — A perfect sphere of compressed nothingness. No one knows what it's made of. It has no detectable interior.

---

**PULSE**
*The most active worlds. The most generous.*

- **Signal Fiber** *(common)* — Raw frequency made tangible. Warm, faintly vibrating. Surfaces constantly on high-activity days.
- **Surge Crystal** *(uncommon)* — Energy crystallized during peak activity windows. Only forms when the community is genuinely alive.
- **Resonance Bloom** *(rare)* — Only surfaces when a server is unusually active. Glows briefly before stabilizing into a permanent form.

---

**ECHO**
*Slow yields. Old materials. Unlike anything from younger worlds.*

- **Memory Dust** *(common)* — Residue from things that happened here before anyone was watching. Fine, persistent, faintly luminescent.
- **Remnant Shard** *(uncommon)* — Fragments of structures that predate the current community. What those structures were has not been determined.
- **Hollow Chime** *(rare)* — Makes no sound. Named for what it used to do. Found only in the deepest layers of Echo geology.

---

**DRIFT**
*Unpredictable. Unfinished. Occasionally remarkable.*

- **Drifting Ore** *(common)* — Loose material that never fully settled into the planet's crust. Easy to find. Hard to keep in one place.
- **Wayward Filament** *(uncommon)* — Thread-like strands that connect nothing to nothing. Their tensile strength is inexplicable.
- **Unmoored Stone** *(rare)* — Dense and heavy. Shouldn't exist this far from a gravity center. Nobody can explain why it's here.

---

**EMBER**
*Reliable. Warm. The kind of planet that takes care of you.*

- **Ember Ash** *(common)* — Fine residue from sustained warmth. Smells like something old. Communities don't notice when they start finding it everywhere.
- **Hearthglass** *(uncommon)* — Formed when community heat compresses over long periods. Clear, warm-toned, difficult to shatter.
- **Deep Cinder** *(rare)* — Only found at the core layer. Takes a long time and a lot of consistent activity to surface. Worth the wait.

---

**STATIC**
*Chaotic yield. No pattern. Pay attention anyway.*

- **Interference Grain** *(common)* — Noise made solid. Slightly unstable. Harmless unless combined with certain other materials.
- **Flux Shard** *(uncommon)* — Unpredictably magnetic. Behaves differently each time you find one. No two specimens are identical.
- **Distortion Prism** *(rare)* — Bends light in ways that don't follow rules. The most sought-after rare material in the galaxy. Static planets guard it without trying.

---

**BLOOM**
*Young planets. Abundant early. Pay attention to the window.*

- **Growth Spore** *(common)* — Early-stage planetary material. Abundant on young worlds. Availability decreases as the planet matures.
- **Tender Filament** *(uncommon)* — New growth compressed into a strand. Delicate. Becomes brittle if the planet ages past Bloom before it's collected.
- **First Bloom Crystal** *(rare)* — Only surfaces in the earliest period of a planet's life. Permanently unavailable once a Bloom world matures. If you weren't there, you missed it.

---

**RELIC**
*Low yield. Ancient materials. The oldest things in the galaxy.*

- **Ancient Grain** *(common)* — Old in a way that common materials usually aren't. Even experienced traders stop when they find one.
- **Fossil Shard** *(uncommon)* — Compressed history. Something is preserved inside but the tools to read it don't exist yet.
- **Origin Fragment** *(rare)* — Pre-dates the framework that would explain it. The oldest material in the known galaxy. Relic worlds yield one every long while. Sometimes less.

---

### Mythic Materials
*Any biome. Any planet. Near-impossible rates.*

These two materials are not associated with any biome. They surface anywhere, to anyone, without warning. No crafting use has been confirmed for either. The galaxy simply produces them occasionally, as if reminding everyone that it contains things that haven't been understood yet.

- **Pulse Ember** — Where Void meets warmth. A material that shouldn't be able to exist in stable form. It does anyway. The rarest confirmed surface event in the known galaxy.
- **???** — Has no official designation. Appears in drop logs as three question marks. No specimen has been publicly documented. Whether it has properties, a use, or a name is unknown. It has been found. That much is confirmed.
