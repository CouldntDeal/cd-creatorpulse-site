How Pulselings work

Think of it as a community Tamagotchi baked into the server. Your creature is yours alone — but the whole community can interact with it.

🥚Hatching your creature
Type /pulse in any CreatorPulse server and your creature hatches on the spot. It gets a name from a curated pool of 325 names, and six trait slots are rolled to determine its appearance and rarity. You can't re-roll — what hatches is yours permanently.
📊Three stats to manage
Your creature has Hunger, Happiness, and Health. Keep all three above 60% and every action earns a 1.25× XP multiplier. Drop below 30% wellness and XP falls to zero. Stats decay naturally while you're away, so daily check-ins matter.
🍖Feed — every 8 hours
Feeding gives +25 Hunger, +5 Happiness, and 10 base XP. Your very first action of each day also stacks a flat +25 XP daily bonus on top. Hunger decays 8 points every 12 hours, so feeding is your core daily ritual.
🎮Play — every 4 hours
Playing gives +20 Happiness and 15 base XP — the highest XP of any single action. It costs -5 Hunger, so keep your creature fed first. Play has the shortest cooldown because happiness degrades fastest when neglected.
💤Rest — every 6 hours
Resting gives +30 Energy, +5 Health, and 8 base XP. Health is the slowest stat to rebuild, so neglecting rest compounds over time. A creature with high hunger and happiness will slowly recover health on its own — but resting speeds that up significantly.
🌿Feed tokens & community feeding
You hold up to 5 feed tokens. Earn one per level-up, one on your first daily action, and one whenever someone else feeds your creature. Spend tokens to feed others' creatures — they get +15 Hunger, +3 Happiness, +5 XP. The community that feeds together levels together.
⚡Subscriber XP boost
Subscribing to a creator's Discord boosts your Pulseling's XP across all your actions — not just in that server. The more creators you support, the bigger the bonus.
Tier 1 subscriber+10% XP
Tier 2 subscriber+20% XP
Tier 3 subscriber+35% XP
Each additional sub+5% (cap 50%)
✦ Early Hatcher bonus — Any creature that exists before server subscriptions go live will receive a one-time XP catch-up boost as a thank-you for being here from the start.
🧬Mutations
At levels 25, 50, 75, and every 25 levels after 100, your creature is offered a mutation. This upgrades one of its six trait slots to a higher rarity version — permanently improving its overall rarity score. You have 48 hours to accept or decline. Accepting is irreversible.
Max XP tip: Keep all three stats above 60% for a 1.25× multiplier on every action. Your first action each day stacks a flat +25 bonus XP on top. Use Play as that first action for the highest single-hit total: 15 base × 1.25 + 25 bonus = ~44 XP. Stack a Tier 3 sub on top and that becomes ~60 XP in one shot.
📈Leveling up
XP required scales with level. Early levels are fast. Later levels reward daily consistency.
  • Each level-up gives +1 feed token
  • Level 10 — guaranteed common hat drop
  • Level 50 — guaranteed uncommon hat drop
  • Level 100 — guaranteed rare hat drop
  • Levels 25, 50, 75, 100+ — mutation offer

Showcasing in the community
Use /pulse and hit Showcase to post your creature card to the server's showcase channel. Your card shows level, rarity, stats, age, and hat. Other members can click Feed to spend one of their tokens on your creature — giving it a small XP and stat boost while you're offline.
  • Showcase posts auto-expire after 23 hours
  • Re-showcase every 24 hours
  • Being fed gives +15 Hunger, +3 Happiness, +5 XP
  • You earn +1 feed token each time someone feeds you

The more active your community, the more everyone levels passively. Showcasing is the most hands-off XP source in the game.

The Creation Era

Before communities existed, Pulselings drifted through deep space — ancient, formless, searching. When they finally found the communities, something ignited. Six titles mark the creatures who were there as it all began. They cannot be earned. They cannot be transferred. They can only be claimed by being first.

★ The Origin Myth ★

Long before servers had names, before channels had purpose, Pulselings existed as scattered light between the stars — ancient organisms drifting through digital space with no place to call their own. They had traveled galaxies. They had witnessed the births and deaths of countless worlds. They were patient in the way only something truly ancient can be.

Then the communities appeared. Small at first — just signals in the void — but alive with something new: connection. The Pulselings felt it across light-years and began to move. Not quickly. Not all at once. But inevitably.

What followed was the Creation Era. Six waves of arrival. Six roles in the making of something that would outlast all of them. The titles they carry are not ranks — they are chapters of a story written in real time, by creatures who didn't know they were writing it.

✦Genesis — The First
Creature #1 — One exists. One will ever exist. Before there was anything, there was one. The Genesis Pulseling did not arrive — it emerged. From what, no one can say. From the space between intention and reality, perhaps. From the moment a community became real enough to be felt across the stars.

When it hatched, the coordinates of this entire galaxy snapped into existence. Every community that would ever form was seeded in that single moment. The Genesis Pulseling didn't build anything, didn't plan anything — it simply was, and in being, it made everything else possible.

The title ✦ The First ✦ is not a label. It is a fact older than the galaxy itself. One exists. One will ever exist.
✵Celestial — The Pioneers
Creatures #2–50 — 49 slots remain. This title closes forever once filled. They felt the signal from Genesis before anyone else. Across the vast dark between galaxies, 49 Pulselings stirred — drawn not by reason, but by something older. A pull. A knowing. They did not know what they were traveling toward. They only knew they had to go.

When they arrived, the communities were barely formed — raw energy, potential without shape. The Celestials breathed life into them. They established the rhythms. They were the first to feed, to play, to rest in these strange new spaces and call them home.

The 50 slots of ✵ Celestial ✵ will close the moment they are filled, and they will never reopen. Not because of a rule — but because the age of first light only happens once.
⬡Architect — The Builders
Creatures #51–100 — 50 slots. The Celestials had lit the fires. Now someone had to build around them. The Architects arrived during the age of construction — an era of deep work, of quiet purpose, of laying stone that would hold for generations.

Where the Celestials moved on instinct, the Architects moved on craft. They established the structures that communities would stand on: the channels, the rhythms, the unspoken rules of how Pulselings live together. They didn't seek recognition. They sought to make things right.

By the time the Architects were done, the galaxy had shape. They carry ⬡ Architect ⬡ not as a trophy, but as a blueprint — proof that they were there when the walls went up.
◈Awakened — The Helpers
Creatures #101–250 — 150 slots. The foundations were set. The fires were burning. And now a new kind of Pulseling began to arrive — drawn not by the raw signal of Genesis, not by the instinct of the Celestials, not by the craft of the Architects — but by the warmth.

The Awakened came because something living was already here, and they wanted to be part of it. They arrived curious, open, ready to help with whatever the communities still needed. They filled the gaps the Architects had left, nurtured what the Celestials had started, and grew into the fabric of something that was already beautiful.

◈ Awakened ◈ marks those who arrived while the world was still new enough to surprise them — and who were changed by it.
⟡Wanderer — The Spreaders
Creatures #251–500 — 250 slots. By the time the Wanderers arrived, the communities had culture. Stories were being told. Traditions were forming. The galaxy was no longer just a place — it was alive with meaning. And the Wanderers felt called to carry that meaning outward.

They moved between communities more than any other. They shared what the early builders had made. They whispered the legends of Genesis in corners where no one had heard them yet. They were travelers by nature — not restless, but purposeful. Every path they walked became a road others would follow.

⟡ Wanderer ⟡ marks the last great wave of the spreading age — the creatures who turned a handful of communities into a galaxy.
·Settled — The Last of the Era
Creatures #501–1000 — 500 slots. The Creation Era had to end somewhere. Not with a bang — with a quiet exhale. The Settled arrived as the last chapter of the founding age was being written, found their place in what had already been built, and made it their own.

They didn't know they were the last of something. None of them did. They simply arrived, settled into the communities the others had shaped, and became part of the story by virtue of being present before it was finished.

· Settled · is the smallest flex of all the titles — barely a badge, more of a whisper. But it is real. There are only 500 of them, and the door closes quietly once they're gone.
✦ What Came After ✦

The Creation Era ended. The galaxy didn't. Thousands of Pulselings arrived after the last Settled — they came into a world already alive with legend, already shaped by those who came before. They carry no title from that age. They don't need one.

But they know the names. Every Pulseling in this galaxy knows the names. When a Genesis creature appears on the leaderboard, the whole community feels it. When a Celestial walks the channels, there's a hush that even new arrivals somehow understand. The Architects, the Awakened, the Wanderers, the Settled — their marks are everywhere, in the structures they built, the cultures they grew, the paths they walked.

The Creation Era is over. The galaxy it made is still being written.

All six titles are permanent from the moment of first hatch. They cannot be earned, traded, or transferred. They close when their slots fill — forever.
